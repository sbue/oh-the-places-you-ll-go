Migratory Patterns of College Graduates

Santiago Buenahora

About the Project

Who am I?

My name is Santiago Buenahora. I am a Junior in Computer Science, and as I look out into post-grad life, I feel as if my career is pushing me towards either San Francisco or New York. As a result of this geographic pressure, I was inspired to explore how different colleges influence migratory patterns for young adults.

What data sources am I using

I used Selenium and Python to scrape data from several Facebook groups. I used the University of Pennsylvania Class of 2015 Facebook Group, the Harvard University Class of 2015 Facebook Group, the Bowdoin College Class of 2015 Facebook Group, the University of Virginia Class of 2015 Facebook Group, and the Virginia State University Class of 2015 Facebook Group. For each of these Facebook groups, I used Python's pandas library to clean the data, remove students who went to other colleges, and create structured data from messy data resulting from scraped html.

What this data product is and why is it useful

This data product is primarily educational. The aim is to give students an understanding in how where they are in college influences where they are geographically in their next stage of life. If some of these students are among the brightest in the country, then we should understand the consequences of geographic clusters.
